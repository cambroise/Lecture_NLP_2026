3  Exercises — LSA and Word2Vec Embeddings

These exercises explore two complementary approaches to building word representations:

  • Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA) — a classical count-based method using SVD on a term-document matrix
  • Word2Vec — a neural prediction-based method using skip-gram with negative sampling

Both methods were introduced in the lecture (word-embedding.qmd). The practical work is in TP/lsa_word2vec.ipynb.

3.1.1 1) Term-document matrix and cosine similarity

Goal: Build a count matrix from a toy corpus and measure word similarity by cosine distance.

Key ideas

  • A corpus of \(D\) documents with a vocabulary of \(|V|\) words defines a \(|V| \times D\) term-document matrix.
  • Raw counts can be weighted (TF-IDF) to reduce the influence of frequent uninformative words.
  • Cosine similarity \(\cos(\mathbf{v}, \mathbf{w}) = \frac{\mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{w}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|\|\mathbf{w}\|}\) is the standard metric between word vectors.

Exercises

  1. Build the term-document matrix with CountVectorizer. What is its shape? How sparse is it?
  2. Switch to TfidfVectorizer. Which words gain weight? Which lose weight?
  3. Compute cosine similarity between pairs of words from the same topic and across topics. Do the numbers match your intuition?

3.1.2 2) Latent Semantic Analysis — SVD and latent space

Goal: Apply Truncated SVD to reduce dimensionality and visualize the latent semantic structure.

Key ideas

  • LSA factorizes the matrix \(W = U \Sigma V^T\) and keeps only the top \(k\) singular values: \(\hat{W} = U_k \Sigma_k V_k^T\).
  • The rows of \(V_k\) are document embeddings; the rows of \(U_k\) (scaled by \(\Sigma_k\)) are word embeddings.
  • Dimensionality reduction acts as denoising: synonymous words that never co-occur in the same document become close in the latent space.

Exercises

  1. Apply TruncatedSVD with \(k=2\) and plot documents in the latent plane. Do the three topics separate cleanly?
  2. Plot the word vectors in the same plane. Which words anchor each dimension?
  3. Increase \(k\) to 5 and 10. How does explained variance change? Use the document embeddings to retrieve the top-3 documents for a free-text query.
  4. Reflection: What does each singular value measure geometrically? Why does LSA struggle with polysemy (e.g. star = celebrity or astronomical object)?

3.1.3 3) Word2Vec on the toy corpus

Goal: Train a skip-gram Word2Vec model and compare its embeddings with LSA.

Key ideas

  • Word2Vec trains a binary classifier: given a (target, context) pair, predict whether the context is real or noise-sampled.
  • The objective \(L = \sum_{(w,c): y=1} \log \sigma(\mathbf{c}^T \mathbf{w}) + \sum_{(w',c'): y=0} \log \sigma(-\mathbf{c'}^T \mathbf{w'})\) is maximized by gradient descent.
  • The context window \(L\) controls what “context” means: small \(L\) captures syntactic relations, large \(L\) captures topical/semantic relations.

Exercises

  1. Train Word2Vec (sg=1, window=3, epochs=200) on the toy corpus. Visualize the embeddings with PCA.
  2. Compare side-by-side: for the same word pairs, are Word2Vec similarities higher/lower/more discriminative than LSA?
  3. Try sg=0 (CBOW) and window=1 vs window=5. What changes in the clustering?
  4. Reflection: Why does Word2Vec underperform on this 18-sentence corpus? What data regime favours LSA?

3.1.4 4) Pre-trained embeddings and analogy tasks

Goal: Load large-scale GloVe vectors and test the famous linear structure of word embeddings.

Key ideas

  • Pre-trained GloVe vectors are trained on Wikipedia + Gigaword (~6 billion tokens, 400k vocabulary, 50–300 dimensions).
  • The linear analogy structure \(\mathbf{king} - \mathbf{man} + \mathbf{woman} \approx \mathbf{queen}\) emerges from training at scale.
  • This structure does not appear in LSA or Word2Vec trained on small corpora.

Exercises

  1. Load glove-wiki-gigaword-50 with gensim.downloader. Solve the analogies: king − man + woman, Paris − France + Italy, walk − walking + swimming.
  2. Try to solve analogies with your toy LSA vectors. What happens?
  3. Find 3 word pairs where GloVe similarities agree with LSA, and 3 where they strongly disagree. What explains the disagreements?

3.1.5 5) Comparison and reflection

Goal: Synthesise the trade-offs between LSA and Word2Vec.

Property LSA Word2Vec
Method SVD on count matrix Neural skip-gram
Training Closed-form Gradient descent
Corpus size needed Small OK Needs billions of words
Polysemy handling Poor (single vector) Poor (single vector)
Analogy structure Weak Strong at scale
Interpretability Moderate (SVD components) Low
Matrix storage \(O(\|V\| \cdot D)\) \(O(\|V\| \cdot d)\)

Exercises

  1. In which practical scenario would you choose LSA over Word2Vec? Give two concrete examples.
  2. Both methods produce a single vector per word regardless of context. Why is this a fundamental limitation? What architecture overcomes it? (Hint: see chapters/transformers.qmd.)
  3. The lecture notes describe PPMI as an alternative to raw counts before SVD. Implement PPMI weighting and compare LSA-PPMI similarity with raw-count LSA and Word2Vec.
